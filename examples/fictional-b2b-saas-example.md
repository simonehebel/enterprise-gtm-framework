# Fictional B2B SaaS GTM Example

## Important Note

This is a fictional example created solely to demonstrate how the frameworks in this repository can be applied.

The company, markets, account data and recommendations are fictional. No confidential employer, customer or proprietary information is included.

---

## 1. Company Context

**Company:** FlowCore

**Business model:** Enterprise B2B SaaS

**Solution:** Process intelligence and workflow optimization software

**Target region:** Europe

**Primary business objective:** Build qualified enterprise pipeline in Germany, the United Kingdom and the Netherlands.

FlowCore helps large organizations identify process inefficiencies, improve operational transparency and prioritize transformation initiatives.

---

## 2. Business Challenge

FlowCore has a strong product and several international customers, but its regional GTM execution is inconsistent.

Current challenges include:

- limited regional prioritization,
- broad and undifferentiated messaging,
- inconsistent Sales and Marketing alignment,
- insufficient account-level engagement,
- limited visibility into pipeline contribution,
- different levels of market readiness across countries.

The company needs a more structured approach to regional investment and enterprise account activation.

---

## 3. Market Prioritization

Each market is assessed using two simplified dimensions:

- market attractiveness,
- organizational readiness.

Scores range from 1 to 5.

| Market | Market Attractiveness | Organizational Readiness | Recommended Decision |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Germany | 5 | 4 | Invest |
| United Kingdom | 4 | 4 | Invest |
| Netherlands | 3 | 3 | Test |

### Germany

Germany offers the strongest combination of enterprise potential, industrial transformation demand and existing Sales coverage.

Recommended approach:

- prioritize large industrial enterprises,
- invest in targeted ABM programs,
- use local customer references,
- develop German-language executive content,
- coordinate closely with regional Sales leadership.

### United Kingdom

The United Kingdom offers strong enterprise software adoption and relatively high category maturity.

Recommended approach:

- prioritize technology, financial services and professional services,
- combine executive events with account-based campaigns,
- use thought leadership to strengthen differentiation,
- activate existing partner relationships.

### Netherlands

The Netherlands shows potential but has limited commercial coverage.

Recommended approach:

- run a focused market validation pilot,
- test demand within a small account cluster,
- use partner-led activation,
- review results before larger investment.

---

## 4. Priority Customer Segment

### Segment

Large manufacturing organizations with:

- more than 2,000 employees,
- multiple enterprise systems,
- complex operational processes,
- active digital transformation initiatives,
- pressure to improve efficiency,
- executive sponsorship for process improvement.

### Segment Rationale

This segment is attractive because:

- operational inefficiency creates measurable cost,
- process transparency is a recognized business priority,
- buying committees include both business and technology stakeholders,
- transformation initiatives often have executive sponsorship,
- the solution can support both strategic and operational outcomes.

---

## 5. Customer Problem

Large organizations often lack a consistent view of how business processes perform across systems, teams and regions.

Typical consequences include:

- process delays,
- duplicated work,
- higher operating costs,
- compliance risk,
- low adoption of transformation initiatives,
- limited ability to prioritize improvements.

### Cost of Inaction

Without improved process visibility, organizations may continue investing in transformation initiatives without clear evidence of where change will create the greatest value.

## 8. Recommended GTM Motion

The recommended GTM model combines three approaches.

### Strategic Accounts: 1:1 ABM

Use for a limited number of high-value enterprise accounts.

Activities may include:

- account research,
- tailored value propositions,
- executive briefings,
- personalized content,
- stakeholder mapping,
- coordinated Sales and Marketing outreach.

### Priority Account Clusters: 1:Few ABM

Use for groups of accounts with similar industry challenges.

Activities may include:

- industry-specific campaigns,
- executive roundtables,
- targeted webinars,
- sector-specific case studies,
- shared nurture programs.

### Broader Market: Demand Generation and Nurture

Use to build awareness and identify buying signals.

Activities may include:

- educational content,
- search campaigns,
- webinars,
- process transformation guides,
- lifecycle nurture,
- account engagement scoring.

## 9. Messaging by Audience

### Chief Operating Officer

**Priority problem:** Operational inefficiency and limited process transparency

**Core message:** Identify where process improvement will create measurable operational value.

### Chief Information Officer

**Priority problem:** Fragmented technology environments and unclear transformation priorities

**Core message:** Turn existing process data into actionable transformation insight.

### Transformation Leader

**Priority problem:** Too many initiatives and limited evidence for prioritization

**Core message:** Focus transformation resources on the processes with the greatest business impact.

### Process Excellence Lead

**Priority problem:** Manual analysis and inconsistent improvement methods

**Core message:** Create a structured and scalable process improvement model.

## 13. Example 90-Day Plan

### First 30 Days

1. Validate market and segment assumptions.
2. Agree priority accounts with Sales.
3. Review available content and proof points.
4. Define qualification and handover criteria.
5. Establish shared KPIs.

### Days 31–60

1. Launch the first 1:Few account cluster campaign.
2. Develop executive-level messaging.
3. Activate a regional webinar or roundtable.
4. Start account engagement reporting.
5. Review Sales follow-up quality.

### Days 61–90

1. Evaluate account and pipeline response.
2. Expand successful messages and channels.
3. Adjust underperforming programs.
4. Decide whether to scale the Netherlands pilot.
5. Present recommendations to regional leadership.

## 14. Key Risks

| Risk | Business Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Weak Sales follow-up | Lost account engagement | Define response times and ownership |
| Generic messaging | Low relevance | Adapt messages by segment and buying role |
| Limited proof points | Reduced credibility | Prioritize references and outcome evidence |
| Poor data quality | Inaccurate targeting | Validate account and contact data |
| Too many priority accounts | Diluted resources | Apply clear account selection criteria |

---

## 15. Recommended Decision

FlowCore should:

1. invest in Germany and the United Kingdom,
2. test the Netherlands through a limited pilot,
3. prioritize large manufacturing organizations,
4. combine 1:1, 1:Few and broader demand programs,
5. establish shared Sales and Marketing account reviews,
6. measure progression from engagement to pipeline.

## Key Learning

The same product should not be marketed identically across all markets, segments or buying roles.

Effective enterprise GTM execution requires alignment between:

- market potential,
- customer need,
- organizational readiness,
- account strategy,
- messaging,
- Sales and Marketing ownership,
- performance measurement.
