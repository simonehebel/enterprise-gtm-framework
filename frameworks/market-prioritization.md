# Market Prioritization Framework

## Purpose

This framework supports evidence-based decisions about which markets, regions or countries should receive go-to-market investment and organizational attention.

It helps leadership teams compare market attractiveness with commercial and operational readiness before allocating budget, resources and executive attention.

## When to Use This Framework

Use this framework when an organization needs to:

- prioritize countries or regions,
- decide where to invest or expand,
- compare market potential with execution readiness,
- allocate Sales and Marketing resources,
- identify markets that require testing before larger investment,
- create transparent and evidence-based GTM decisions.

## Assessment Dimensions

Each market should be assessed across six dimensions.

### 1. Market Attractiveness

Evaluate the overall commercial potential of the market.

Questions to consider:

- How large is the addressable market?
- How quickly is the market growing?
- How mature is the product category?
- Is there a clear and urgent customer need?
- Are regulatory or industry developments creating demand?

### 2. Customer Potential

Evaluate the quality and relevance of the available customer opportunity.

Questions to consider:

- How many relevant target accounts exist?
- What is the expected average account value?
- How strongly does the solution address customer priorities?
- Is there evidence of buying readiness?
- Is there potential for expansion or cross-selling?

### 3. Competitive Position

Evaluate the organization's ability to compete effectively.

Questions to consider:

- How strong are existing competitors?
- Is the solution sufficiently differentiated?
- How well known is the brand?
- Are local customer references available?
- What barriers to entry exist?

### 4. Commercial Readiness

Evaluate whether the organization can successfully sell and support the solution.

Questions to consider:

- Is sufficient Sales coverage available?
- Are relevant partners established?
- Is Customer Success capacity available?
- Is the pricing model suitable for the market?
- How complex is the procurement environment?

### 5. Marketing Readiness

Evaluate whether Marketing can create and support demand effectively.

Questions to consider:

- Are relevant customer insights available?
- Is suitable content available?
- What localization is required?
- Are effective channels accessible?
- Are the necessary data and technology capabilities available?

### 6. Execution Feasibility

Evaluate whether the strategy can realistically be implemented.

Questions to consider:

- Is sufficient budget available?
- Are the required internal capabilities available?
- Do stakeholders support the market strategy?
- Are there legal or operational constraints?
- What is the expected time to measurable impact?

## Suggested Scoring Model

Score each dimension from 1 to 5.

| Score | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 1 | Very weak |
| 2 | Weak |
| 3 | Moderate |
| 4 | Strong |
| 5 | Very strong |

The scoring should not replace management judgment. Its purpose is to create transparency, identify assumptions and support structured decision-making.

## Recommended Decision Categories

### Invest

The market offers strong commercial potential and the organization has sufficient capabilities to execute.

### Build

The market is strategically attractive, but important capabilities, partnerships or local foundations still need to be developed.

### Test

The opportunity appears promising but requires validation through a limited pilot or targeted campaign.

### Maintain

The existing presence should be supported, but significant additional investment is not currently justified.

### Deprioritize

The market currently offers limited potential, insufficient strategic fit or unacceptable execution risk.

## Required Output

For each assessed market, document:

- priority category,
- strategic rationale,
- target customer segments,
- required investment,
- critical dependencies,
- key risks,
- accountable owner,
- next review or decision date.

## Example Decision Summary

| Market | Attractiveness | Readiness | Decision |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Market A | 5 | 4 | Invest |
| Market B | 4 | 2 | Build |
| Market C | 3 | 3 | Test |
| Market D | 2 | 2 | Deprioritize |

## Key Principle

A large market is not automatically a priority market.

Investment decisions should balance:

- commercial potential,
- customer relevance,
- competitive strength,
- organizational readiness,
- execution feasibility.
